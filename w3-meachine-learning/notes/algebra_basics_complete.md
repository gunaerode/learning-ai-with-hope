# Like Terms and Unlike Terms

These concepts are used when we add or subtract algebraic expressions.

---

## 1. LIKE TERMS

**Like terms** are terms that have:

- the same variables  
- raised to the same powers  

Only the numerical coefficients can be different.

### Examples of Like Terms

- 3x and 5x  
- 7a and -2a  
- 4xy and 9xy  
- 6x^2 and -3x^2  

These are like terms because the variable part is exactly the same.

---

### Collection of Like Terms

Like terms can be combined (collected) together by addition or subtraction.

#### Examples

- 3x + 5x = 8x  
- 7a - 2a = 5a  
- 4xy + 9xy = 13xy  
- 6x^2 - 3x^2 = 3x^2  

This process is called **collection of like terms**.

---

## 2. UNLIKE TERMS

**Unlike terms** are terms that have:

- different variables, or  
- different powers  

### Examples of Unlike Terms

- 3x and 4y  
- 5x and 5x^2  
- 7a and 2b  
- 3xy and 3x  

These terms have different variable parts or different powers.

---

### Important Rule

**Unlike terms cannot be collected**

They must remain as they are.

#### Examples

- 3x + 4y â†’ cannot be simplified  
- 5x + 2x^2 â†’ cannot be simplified  

---

## Summary

| Type | Definition | Example | Can be Collected? |
|------|------------|---------|-------------------|
| Like Terms | Same variable and same power | 3x and 5x | YES |
| Unlike Terms | Different variable or power | 3x and 4y | NO |

---

### Simple Rule to Remember

- Same variable and same power = Like Terms  
- Different variable or different power = Unlike Terms
